Basics and Gameplay

  • The objective of the game is to beat the dealer by getting your score as close as possible to 21 without going over 21.
  • Your score is the sum of the values of your cards. Number cards are worth their face value, face cards are worth 10, and Aces are worth 1 or 11, depending on what's best (this game will calculate that automatically).
  • You start off with $5,000 in chips and must place a bet before every game. Once you've decided on your bet, click the "Deal" button to start the initial deal.

The Initial Deal

  • The dealer will deal out four cards: two to you and two to himself. However, the dealer's second card will be face-down, so you don't know his exact score.
  • If you get exactly 21 right away, that's called Blackjack, and you win 1.5 times your original bet.
  • If the dealer has Blackjack, you lose your original bet. If you both have Blackjack, it's a push, and you don't win or lose anything.
  • If nobody has Blackjack, the game will continue.

Hitting and Standing

  • Once the initial deal is complete, you can choose to "Hit" or "Stand".
  • If you choose to "Hit", you will receive one additional card. If you choose to "Stand", you will not receive any additional cards.
  • You can hit as many times as you want before you stand, but if you go over 21, you bust and lose automatically.
  • If you're still under 21 and choose to stand, the dealer's turn will begin.

Doubling Down

  • Instead of hitting or standing, you can also double down.
  • If you do, your initial bet will be doubled, you'll automatically be dealt one more card, and the game ends.
  • The dealer will show his cards, and you will not have any more opportunities to hit or stand.
  • Only double down if you think you can win with exactly one more card!

Insurance

  • If the dealer has an Ace showing, his chances of getting Blackjack are higher.
  • To protect yourself against this, you can buy insurance, which costs half your original bet.
  • If the dealer does have Blackjack, you win your insurance bet and keep your original bet, so you break even and the game ends.
  • If the dealer does not have Blackjack, you lose your insurance bet and the game continues with your original bet.

Splitting

  • If you get two cards of the same value, you can split them into two separate hands.
  • Your bet will be doubled, and you will be dealt one additional card for each hand to make it complete.
  • You will take turns hitting or standing for each hand, just like in the initial deal.
  • You can either lose on both hands, win on both hands, or win on one hand and lose on the other.
